Starting in late April 2021, the Emergency Broadband Benefit can help you pay for your broadband internet costs.

What is it?

  • $50 a month off your broadband internet bill
  • $75 a month if you live on Tribal lands
  • $100 towards the purchase of a laptop or tablet

Am I eligible?

You are eligible if someone in your household:

  • Had a substantial loss of income in the last year
  • Gets Medi-Cal
  • Gets EBT (CalFresh or CalWORKS) benefits
  • Gets school lunch through the free and reduced price school lunch program
  • Got a Pell Grant this year
  • Qualifies for Lifeline phone benefits
  • Qualifies for an internet provider’s existing low-income (or COVID-19) broadband program

How do I sign up?

You can sign up directly with a broadband provider once the program starts. To see which broadband providers will offer discounts in your community, enter your address below. (idea of what this could be: food bank search)

How long with this program last?

This is a temporary program and federal government is only paying for these discounts until the money for the program is spent or the pandemic has ended.

Your broadband provider will tell you right away if the program is ending and your provider will provide options for other low-cost broadband programs, if they are available.

Your broadband service will end unless you let your broadband provider know that you want to continue paying for a more expensive broadband plan once the program ends.

How do I get more questions answered or tell you what I think about the program?

Let us know your questions and we’ll add them to our list here.

If you have comments or ideas about the program, we’d like to hear them. The federal government made the rules for this program, and we want to make sure it works well for Californians.
