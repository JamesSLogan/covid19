Vaccine administration overview

Here are all vaccinations administered in California, by county of residence. Statewide vaccines administered combine all the county numbers.

The vaccine doses administered under the federal pharmacy program are not included in the statewide count. This program provides vaccines directly to the pharmacy and are counted as part of the overall doses delivered to the state.

This is updated daily.

  • Doses delivered include the CDC Long Term Care (LTC) Pharmacy Partnership Program, Federal Retail Pharmacy Partnership Program, Federal Dialysis Center Program, Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) partner sites, and Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA) partner sites.
  • This does not include doses delivered to the following federal agencies: Indian Health Service, Veterans Health Administration, Department of Defense, and Federal Bureau of Prisons. The CDC LTC Pharmacy and Federal Retail Pharmacy Partnership Program doses are a subset of the doses delivered.
  • Partially-vaccinated people are those receiving only one dose of the Pfizer or Moderna vaccine. Fully-vaccinated people are those who received two doses of the Pfizer or Moderna vaccines or the Janssen vaccine.
  • Where the county of residence was not reported, the county where vaccinated is used. This applies to less than 1% of vaccination records. The sum of county-level vaccinations does not equal statewide total vaccinations because some out-of-state residents are vaccinated in California.

Vaccinations progress by group

These charts show you cumulatively, since we began vaccinating, how much we’ve been able to vaccinate priority groups and communities hardest hit. These are updated weekly.

The California Healthy Places Index (HPI), developed by the Public Health Alliance of Southern California, explores local factors that predict life expectancy and compares community conditions across the state. The HPI quartiles are the total state population divided into 25% segments based on conditions that shape health, like housing, transportation, education, and more.

The Vaccine Equity Metric (VEM) combines HPI with CDPH-derived scores. ZIP Codes range from less healthy community conditions in Quartile 1 to more healthy community conditions in Quartile 4.
