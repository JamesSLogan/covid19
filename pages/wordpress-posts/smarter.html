The SMARTER Plan is the next phase of California’s COVID-19 response.

On this page:

  • What SMARTER stands for
  • How we’ll move forward
  • How we’ll measure success
  • Read the details

What SMARTER stands for

We’re using the lessons of the last two years to prepare for future COVID-19 surges or variants.

SMARTER stands for:

  • Shots – Vaccines are our most powerful weapon against hospitalization and serious illness.
  • Masks – Masks help slow the spread of the virus.
  • Awareness – We will keep tracking COVID-19, communicating how to protect yourself, and coordinating state and local efforts.
  • Readiness – We will be ready with tools, resources, and supplies to respond to emerging situations.
  • Testing – We will get tests to where they’re needed most.
  • Education – We will work to keep schools open and kids in classrooms for in-person instruction.
  • Rx – Evolving and improved treatments will be an important tool in saving lives

The SMARTER Plan is not just for COVID-19. We can use these strategies and systems for future emergencies. We will improve the SMARTER Plan as we learn what works.

How we’ll move forward

COVID-19 is still here. We’ll adapt to make sure our response is the right one for future surges.

Track the right data

We will identify and track the right kind of data for each COVID-19 variant. We’ll use this data to determine how we’ll respond.

For example, if there’s a highly infectious variant that does not cause severe illness, we may focus on hospital numbers. This will help us keep our healthcare system from being overwhelmed. If there’s a variant that causes severe illness, we may track case numbers. In this case, preventing cases could save lives.

Stay home when you’re sick

If you test positive for COVID-19 or feel sick, stay at home.

If you’re exposed to someone with COVID-19:

  • Consider getting tested
  • Wear a mask until you are sure you’re not infected

Adjust isolation and quarantine times based on science

We will study variants to understand how long someone can infect others. Based on this data, we will adjust how long people with or suspected of having COVID-19 should stay at home.

Wear masks when needed

There may be times when masks will save lives. When the situation improves, we will loosen masking requirements.

Take advantage of new treatments

We will continue to adopt and build on innovations in healthcare, like:

  • New pharmaceutical treatments
  • Encouraging outdoor gatherings
  • Improvements in telehealth

How we’ll measure success

We’ve set goals as part of the SMARTER Plan.
